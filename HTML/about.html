About Dev-Maze

Hello! My name is Nathan Russo, and I am thrilled you are checking out my game.

 I have always enjoyed making mazes. I would draw massive ones in the sand at the beach. During my 1st year, I participated in CSH Hacks. During that event, I decided to generate version 0 of Dev-Maze. It was written in Python, using Turtle. It generated one maze and allowed the player (turtle) to move from the start corner to the end corner.

 As a member of CSH, I must create a major project once a year. Using the idea from CSH Hacks, I decided to make Dev-Maze proper on CSH's arcade machine Devcade. Dev-Maze was written in C# and MonoGame. The game generates random, loop-erased mazes, using a variation of Wilson's Maze Algorithm. The player can select 9 different maze sizes from a 1x2 to a 40x80.

 After completing Dev-Maze for my 1st year project, I had one reoccurring issue. Non-CSH members could not access my game. So, for my 2nd year project, I decided to recreate Dev-Maze through a website to allow anyone to play the game. Unlike before, Dev-Maze Website is written in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Thank you for reading the history of Dev-Maze and I hope you enjoy.
